Provider profiles
OpenAI (ChatGPT / GPT)
Market leader with the widest enterprise integrations. Best for coding, document generation, and general reasoning. Premium pricing but extensive ecosystem via Azure. ChatGPT Pro is $200/seat/month.
Anthropic (Claude)
Safety-first design. Excellent for long document analysis, legal/compliance review, and nuanced writing. Haiku = cheap volume work. Sonnet = everyday business use. Opus = deep high-stakes analysis.
Google (Gemini)
Best choice if already on Google Workspace or GCP. Gemini Enterprise is $30/user/month for unlimited access. Leads some intelligence benchmarks. Strong multimodal capabilities (images + text together).
DeepSeek
Chinese startup, open-source model. Up to 97% cheaper than GPT-5 for identical tasks. Can be self-hosted to eliminate API costs entirely. Trade-off: data sovereignty risk if operated on China-based servers.
What is a token?
You type a prompt
→
AI splits it into tokens
→
AI replies in tokens
→
You pay for all tokens used
A token is roughly ¾ of a word. "Hello, how are you?" = ~6 tokens. A typical email ≈ 200 tokens. A 10-page report ≈ 5,000 tokens. 1 million tokens ≈ 750,000 words — about 12 full-length novels. You pay separately for tokens you send in (input) and tokens the AI writes back (output).
Input vs Output — why this drives your bill
Input tokens — cheaper
Everything you send to the AI: your question, documents you paste, instructions, conversation history. Typically 2–5× cheaper than output. Pasting a long contract to summarise = mostly input.
Output tokens — pricier
Everything the AI writes back. If you ask for a 3-page report, those pages cost more than your question did. Shorter, more direct outputs = meaningful cost savings at scale.

Subscription vs API — the $0 vs scale decision: Flat subscriptions are easier to budget and need no engineering. APIs are cheaper at scale but require integration work. Rule of thumb: fewer than ~20 users → subscriptions. Building a product, automation, or customer-facing tool → API pricing compounds savings fast.
Match the job to the right model tier
Customer support chatbot
High volume, routine questions. Speed and cost matter more than reasoning depth.
BudgetHaiku, Flash, or Grok Fast. Cost per query: fractions of a cent.
Contract & legal review
Long documents, nuanced clause extraction, risk flagging. Errors are expensive.
PremiumClaude Opus or GPT-5. Mistakes here cost more than the API bill.
Internal knowledge base
Employees querying company policies, HR docs, product manuals. Medium complexity.
BalancedClaude Sonnet or Gemini Pro. Good quality, manageable cost.

10 rules for smarter AI spending
1
Don't default to the most expensive model. 70% of enterprise tasks work fine on a budget model. Reserve premium tiers for decisions where errors are costly and quality directly impacts outcomes.
2
Output tokens cost 3–5× more than input. If a vendor proposes a solution that generates large AI responses, ask them to engineer for shorter outputs — this directly and immediately cuts your costs.
3
Subscriptions vs API is a volume decision. Under ~20 users? Flat subscriptions are simpler and easier to budget. Building an automation or product for hundreds of users? API pricing scales dramatically better.
4
Ask about prompt caching discounts. Anthropic's Claude charges less when the same document or context is queried repeatedly (e.g. a legal contract asked 50 questions about). This can cut costs by 50%+ on document-heavy workflows.
5
On Google Workspace? Gemini Enterprise is the obvious choice. $30/user/month for unlimited access across all Google AI tools — this beats per-token billing for high-usage teams already inside the Google ecosystem.
6
DeepSeek can cut costs by 95% — but check data sovereignty. If your data flows through Chinese-operated servers, that's a compliance and IP risk. Only use for non-sensitive internal workloads, or insist on a self-hosted deployment.
7
Longer context windows cost more per call. Sending an entire 100-page document every time a user asks one question is expensive. Ask your tech team about "chunking" strategies that send only the relevant pages.
8
Benchmark on your actual data before committing. Run 100 real tasks through 2–3 shortlisted models. Compare accuracy, speed, and cost. The top-ranked model by industry benchmarks may not win on your specific business data.
9
AI prices are falling fast — avoid long lock-ins. GPT-4o pricing dropped ~75% between 2023–2025. Negotiate annual contracts, not 3-year. Ensure your architecture lets you swap providers without rebuilding everything from scratch.
10
Model quality ≠ model vendor. Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google run close at the top tier. Your decision should weigh: data privacy terms, existing cloud agreements (AWS/Azure/GCP discounts apply to AI too), support quality, and realistic switching costs — not just benchmark scores.
