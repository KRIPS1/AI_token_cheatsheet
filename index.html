Providers
Who's who in the market.
Four serious vendors. Each has a clear strength — and a clear reason not to be your default for everything.
AI
OpenAI — ChatGPT / GPT
Market leader with the widest enterprise integrations. Best for coding, document generation, and general reasoning. Premium pricing but deep Azure ecosystem means most enterprises already have a procurement path. ChatGPT Pro costs $200/seat/month.
A
Anthropic — Claude
Safety-first design with the strongest data privacy commitments. Excellent for long document analysis, legal review, compliance work, and nuanced writing. Haiku = cheap volume. Sonnet = everyday business. Opus = high-stakes deep analysis.
Google — Gemini
Best choice if already on Google Workspace or GCP. Gemini Enterprise is $30/user/month for unlimited access. Leads several intelligence benchmarks and strong on multimodal tasks (images + text). Google Workspace discounts stack on GCP billing.
DS
DeepSeek
Chinese startup with open-source models up to 97% cheaper than GPT-5. Can be self-hosted to eliminate API costs entirely. Key trade-off: data sovereignty risk if operated on China-based servers. Only use for non-sensitive internal workloads.
The billing unit
What exactly
is a token?
AI models don't count words — they count tokens. Understanding this is the key to understanding every AI invoice your team will receive.
✏️
You write a prompt
Your question or task
⚙️
Split into tokens
~¾ of a word each
🤖
AI processes & replies
In tokens too
💳
You are billed
Input + output tokens
i
A token ≈ ¾ of a word. "Hello, how are you?" = ~6 tokens. A typical business email = ~200 tokens. A 10-page report = ~5,000 tokens. 1 million tokens ≈ 750,000 words — about 12 full-length novels. You pay separately for tokens you send in (input) and tokens the AI writes back (output).
Input — cheaper
What you send in
Your question, any documents you paste, instructions, and conversation history. Typically 2–5× cheaper than output. Pasting a long contract for analysis = mostly input cost.
Output — pricier
What the AI writes back
Everything the AI generates. Ask for a 3-page summary and those pages cost significantly more than your short question did. Shorter, directed outputs = meaningful savings at scale.

📄
Processing 1,000 contracts (5 pages each) for clause extraction: $2–8 on budget tier vs $80–200 on premium. Match quality requirements to cost — don't overpay for repetitive extraction.

Application guide
The right tier
for every business task.
Overspending on AI usually means using a premium model where a budget one would deliver identical results. Here's how to match tasks to tiers.
Customer support chatbot
High volume, routine questions. Speed and cost matter more than depth of reasoning.
BudgetHaiku, Flash, or Grok Fast. Cost per query: fractions of a cent.
Contract & legal review
Long documents, clause extraction, risk flagging. Errors are expensive here.
PremiumClaude Opus or GPT-5. Mistakes cost more than the API bill.
Internal knowledge base
Staff querying HR docs, policies, product manuals. Medium complexity queries.
BalancedClaude Sonnet or Gemini Pro. Quality without premium cost.
Code generation & dev tools
Developers writing, reviewing, or debugging code. Speed and accuracy both matter.
BalancedGPT-5 or Claude Sonnet. GitHub Copilot = $19/user/month.
Marketing content at scale
Product descriptions, ad copy, social posts in bulk. Volume is what drives spend.
BudgetBudget tier or DeepSeek. Quality sufficient; savings compound at scale.
Board & financial analysis
Synthesising reports, earnings analysis, scenario modelling. High-stakes output.
PremiumClaude Opus or Gemini Ultra. Invest here — output drives major decisions.
Document data extraction
Pulling structured data from invoices, PDFs, forms at volume. Repetitive task.
BudgetDeepSeek or Haiku. Structured repetitive tasks need no premium reasoning.
HR & recruiting
Summarising CVs, drafting JDs, initial screening questions. Moderate complexity.
BalancedGemini Enterprise at $30/seat if HR already uses Google Workspace.
Decision framework
10 decisions that
sharpen AI spending.
Distilled from the most common expensive mistakes enterprises make when deploying AI. Apply these before signing any AI vendor contract.
1
Don't default to the most expensive model
70% of enterprise tasks work fine on a budget model. Reserve premium tiers for decisions where errors are costly and quality directly impacts outcomes.
2
Output tokens cost 3–5× more than input
Ask vendors to engineer for shorter AI responses. This is a direct lever that immediately reduces monthly costs without changing output quality.
3
Subscriptions vs API is a volume decision
Under ~20 users, flat subscriptions are simpler. Building an automation or product for many users? API pricing scales dramatically better.
4
Ask about prompt caching discounts
Anthropic's Claude charges less when the same document context is queried repeatedly. For document-heavy workflows this can cut costs by over 50%.
5
On Google Workspace? Gemini Enterprise is obvious
$30/user/month for unlimited AI access. For high-usage teams already in the Google ecosystem this beats per-token billing significantly.
6
DeepSeek saves 95% — but check sovereignty
If data flows through China-based servers that's a compliance and IP risk. Only use for non-sensitive workloads, or insist on a self-hosted deployment.
7
Longer context windows cost more per call
Sending a 100-page document every time someone asks one question is expensive. Ask your team about "chunking" strategies that send only the relevant pages.
8
Benchmark on your actual data before committing
Run 100 real business tasks through 2–3 shortlisted models. The top industry benchmark winner may not win on your specific business workflows.
9
AI prices are falling — avoid long lock-ins
GPT-4o pricing dropped ~75% from 2023–2025. Negotiate annual, not 3-year contracts. Build so you can swap providers without a full rebuild.
10
Model quality ≠ model vendor
Top models from Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google are highly competitive. Decide on: data privacy terms, existing cloud discounts, support quality, and switching costs.
